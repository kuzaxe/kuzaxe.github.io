Working at Magnet

The University of Toronto - Mississauga Campus (UTM) offers PEY - Professional Experience Year, where students can take a year off to go get work experience in their field. After completing my third year, I moved to Waterloo to join Magnet Forensics as a Software Development Intern. This company has a valuable mission, strong values, and an amazing culture. The 16-month work term flew by and it was an honour to be able to write code that made an impact in the digital forensics field.

Responsibilities

As a member of the artifacts team, my responsibilities included adding support for existing and new artifacts. Artifacts include any application on your phone, computer, or cloud. I would research what data an artifact collects and how it stores it. Then I would talk with examiners to see what information they'd find useful and proceed to develop code that would extract that info. For some, it involves writing SQL queries to parse multiple databases and for others, I would have to go through the hex bytes and 'carve' out the data. Primary programming languages were C# and Python for backend development and scripting.

In my free time, I would help our DataGen team (responsible for researching artifact architecture and creating mock data)with their tasks or work on automating parts of our workflow. And table tennis, cannot forget about that.
